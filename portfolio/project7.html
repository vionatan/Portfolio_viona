Design a mobile shopping app for a craft store

crafTy is an app dedicated to craft supply shopping, serves as a haven for customers seeking distinctive and high-quality supplies for their artistic endeavors. The primary focus is on catering to the needs of passionate artists and craft enthusiast.

The problem and goal

Artists and craft enthusiast needed accessibility to unique craft supplies. As such I thought why not create a craft supply shopping app that allow users to source for unique craft supplies

User research

User Interview

Based on the problem and goal, I jot down some key questions for my user interview:

and with that I gathered 5 user interview results from friends and family and group them based on similar answers and identify their pain points.

One key user segment that emerged from the research comprises busy artists who struggles to find craft supplies that meet their specific requirements and standards. Another key user segment would be educational teachers who are looking out for new and exciting craft supplies that are available for a wide range of age levels. Additionally, the many factors that hindered them from looking for high-quality craft supplies such as personal commitments, budget, or difficulties associated with acquiring supplies in-store.

Pain Points

With the given factors contributing to the research problem, I have gathered a few pain points from the interview script:


Accessibility


Unable to find certain craft supplies in store / supplies that suit their needs

Time


Personal commitments stops them from going in-store to look for craft supplies

Cost


Have to compare multiple craft supplies until it fits their price range

Usability Testing and Finding

With the low-fidelity prototype, I decided to conduct the first usability testing to gather some feedbacks from my friends and family to improve the user experience and needs. The prototype showcases the main flow of the app which is purchasing a craft supply.

These are the tasks and question that the users had to complete during the testing:

1 Search for a craft supply using the search bar
2 Search for a craft supply using the filter option and filter by price
3 would you prefer to search using a search bar or using a filter option?
4 view a craft supply
5 Proceed to purchase the craft supply
6 Confirm your order and complete the payment process

After the user testing, I retrieved all the answers from the interview notes and subsequently categorising them based on the app's features and grouping together those that exhibited similar responses.

By analysing all the responses, here are my finding to help improve the crafTy app:

1 Users want ability to select variations of a product
2 Users want a way to add payment details before the payment process
3 Users need better indication when the search results are loaded
4 Users need more filtering options to enhance their search results
5 Users wants courier option for safer purchase experience
6 The filter options and button names lack clarity, causing user confusion.

Key Takeaways

1

By providing and improving on the filter feature, it gives users more control over their product searches. In addition, choosing an easy navigation layout within the app helps them to navigate easily.

2

It a good habit to iterate on each screen multiple times as you need. With each iterations, the design get betters with users' needs in mind. It is also important to consider the accessibility for users with different range of abilities (e.g. one arm, visual impairment)
